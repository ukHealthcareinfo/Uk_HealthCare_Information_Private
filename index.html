Visa Types: Healthcare & Worker Visas

Healthcare Visa

For professionals in the healthcare sector including nurses, carers, and doctors. Valid for 1–5 years.

  • Minimum Salary: £20,000 – £25,000
  • Job Offer Required
  • English Language Proficiency (IELTS or OET)
  • Can bring family

Skilled Worker Visa

For skilled professionals such as engineers, IT professionals, and teachers. Valid for 2–5 years.

  • Minimum Salary: £25,600 or more
  • Licensed Employer Sponsorship Required
  • English Proficiency Required
  • Route to Permanent Residency

Company Stamp/Letter Requirements

The company stamp/letter is an essential document required for your UK healthcare or worker visa application. This letter verifies the authenticity of your job offer and sponsorship from a licensed UK employer.

The company stamp/letter should include the following details:

  • Job Title, Salary, and Terms of Employment: The letter should clearly state your job role, salary, and terms of employment as agreed with the employer.
  • Employer Name, Address, and Registration Number: The company stamp should include the employer's full name, registered address, and UK company registration number.
  • Statement of Visa Sponsorship: This statement confirms that the employer is sponsoring your visa and is licensed by the UK government to do so.
  • Duration of Employment: The letter must mention how long the employer intends to employ you in the role.
  • Official Company Stamp: The letter must bear the official company stamp to prove it has been issued by the company and is a legal document.

The company stamp/letter is not only necessary for visa approval but also serves as proof of a legitimate offer, ensuring your work visa process moves smoothly.

Advantages of the Company Stamp/Letter

  • Authenticity Confirmation: The company stamp/letter ensures that your job offer is genuine and that you are sponsored by a legitimate, licensed UK employer. This strengthens your visa application.
  • Streamlined Visa Process: By providing a clear and valid job offer along with the stamp/letter, the UK Home Office can quickly verify your employment and approve your visa application.
  • Employer’s Support: It provides proof that the employer is legally supporting your visa application, reducing the risk of rejection.
  • Career Opportunity Validation: It gives you the confidence that you are entering a well-established company and have secured a real job in the UK, which can further help with your integration into the UK workforce.
  • Compliance with UK Regulations: Having this stamp/letter demonstrates that your employer is compliant with UK immigration regulations, ensuring your visa remains valid without complications.

Disadvantages of the Company Stamp/Letter

  • Possible Delays: The process of obtaining a company stamp/letter might take time. Employers may not provide it immediately, which could delay the visa application process.
  • Employer Dependency: If there is any issue with the employer, such as them not being able to provide the correct documentation or failing to meet the UK visa sponsor requirements, your visa process could be jeopardized.
  • Risk of Fraudulent Practices: Some employers may attempt to provide fake company letters, especially when offering jobs to international workers. This could lead to visa rejection or legal issues.
  • Employer Reluctance: In some cases, employers might be reluctant to provide an official stamp/letter if they are unsure about the visa process or if they do not fully understand their responsibilities in sponsoring a worker.

Tips for Handling the Company Stamp/Letter

  • Clear Communication with Employer: Make sure to ask for the letter well in advance. Discuss with your employer what specific information needs to be included in the letter so they can prepare it accordingly.
  • Request an Official Template: Many employers have a standard template for the company letter/stamp. Ask your employer to use this template to ensure all necessary details are included.
  • Verify the Employer’s Sponsor Status: Ensure that the employer is a registered and licensed sponsor for visas. You can check the official UK government register of licensed sponsors to confirm this.
  • Double-Check Details: Before submitting the company stamp/letter as part of your visa application, carefully review it for accuracy, such as the correct job title, salary, and company details. Any inconsistencies might lead to delays or rejection.
  • Follow Up Regularly: If there are delays in receiving the stamp/letter, follow up with the employer to ensure it is issued on time, so your visa application does not get delayed.
  • Use a Professional Template: If the employer does not have a template, you may want to provide a professional template for them to use, which can simplify the process.

Spouse & Family Visa Rules

If your wife is the main visa holder (Healthcare/Skilled Worker), the husband can apply as a dependent.

Husband’s Rules and Rights:

  • Can work full-time in the UK (any legal job)
  • Can apply for National Insurance Number
  • Can travel in and out of the UK
  • Must have valid visa linked to wife’s visa

Documents Required for Husband:

  • Marriage Certificate
  • Passport
  • Wife’s BRP and visa details
  • Proof of address and relationship

Leave to Visit Home Country:

  • Yes, the husband can travel to home country
  • Must inform UK employer if working
  • Should not exceed allowed stay outside the UK (usually 180 days/year)

Interview Preparation for UK Healthcare Jobs

Most UK healthcare companies conduct online interviews before issuing a job offer or Certificate of Sponsorship (CoS). This is a critical part of the hiring process, especially for candidates outside the UK.

Essential Interview Requirements

  • Good internet connection and working camera/mic
  • Formal dress (even for online interviews)
  • Original documents (Passport, Certificates, IELTS/OET, Experience letters)
  • Updated CV in UK format

Common Interview Questions

  • Tell me about yourself
  • Why do you want to work in the UK?
  • How do you handle difficult patients?
  • Describe your past job roles

Bonus Tips

  • Research the NHS or employer website
  • Practice English speaking
  • Be calm and confident

After Interview

  • Receive Job Offer if selected
  • Additional documents may be requested
  • Certificate of Sponsorship issued for visa
