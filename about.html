1. Employment Contracts (Offer Letters)

UK healthcare companies (like NHS or private healthcare organizations) usually offer jobs by sending:

  • Official offer letters or employment contracts.
  • These documents are typically sent via email or PDF.
  • No stamp paper is required in the UK for employment contracts.

In the UK, employment law does not require the use of stamp papers for employment contracts.

Key Point: Employers in the UK only need a job offer letter for visa applications or work permits. This is typically sent via email or PDF, not requiring a stamp paper.

Private Recruitment Agencies:

Private recruitment agencies that help place candidates in UK jobs sometimes ask candidates to sign a stamp paper or letter as part of their formal recruitment process. These documents are typically used to ensure that the candidate and the agency are both legally bound to the terms and conditions of the job placement.

Note: This practice is more common in private agencies rather than public sector employers (like the NHS).

Note: Ensure that you review any documents carefully before signing. Always verify the authenticity of the agency or employer before agreeing to any terms. Remember, stamp papers are rarely used in official UK government healthcare employment and are mostly a requirement for certain recruitment agencies.
